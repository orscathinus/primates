Form, function, and behavior

A body built by branches

Primate evolution is not defined by one trait. It is a shifting mosaic of vision, grasping, locomotion, diet, development, cognition, and social life.

Forward-facing eyesGrasping handsFlexible behavior

Mosaic evolution

Traits do not arrive as a package.

Different branches combine ancestral and derived features in ways that resist a simple march toward modern humans.

~25 million years ago

Apes lose the external tail

Tail loss distinguishes living apes from monkeys, but did not automatically produce upright walking, large brains, or humanlike hands.

At least 6 million years ago

Bipedal traits appear early

Early hominins retained climbing adaptations while also showing evidence associated with upright posture and movement.

Especially after ~2 million years ago

Brains and life histories shift

Brain expansion in Homo unfolded alongside changes in diet, technology, development, ranging, and social learning.

The scientific toolkit

One question, many kinds of evidence

Researchers build evolutionary explanations by asking whether independent evidence converges on the same pattern.

01

Comparative anatomy

Shared structures and functional differences reveal homology, adaptation, and possible relationships.

02

Geologic dating

Radiometric methods, stratigraphy, and paleomagnetism place fossils within time ranges.

03

Genomics

DNA comparisons clarify living relationships, population mixtures, and approximate divergence times.

04

Paleoenvironment

Plant remains, isotopes, sediments, and associated animals reconstruct habitats and ecological pressures.
