A better mental model

Evolution branches, overlaps, and leaves gaps.

Fossils are samples from a vast, incomplete record. Many featured species are close relatives—not proven direct ancestors—and scientific interpretations change when new evidence appears.

01
⌁

No living species is “primitive”

Lemurs, monkeys, apes, and humans have all been evolving for the same amount of time since their shared ancestors.

02
⑂

Traits evolve in mosaics

Grasping hands, stereoscopic vision, larger brains, tail loss, and bipedalism appeared at different times and in different combinations.

03
◌

Uncertainty is part of the science

Dates are often ranges, family-tree positions can be debated, and a single new skeleton can rearrange old hypotheses.

Deep-time overview

Four eras. Countless experiments.

Move from the shadow of the dinosaurs through tropical radiations, the age of apes, and the recent spread of our own genus.

66–56 Ma

Paleocene

Near the roots

Plesiadapiform mammals occupied arboreal niches soon after the end-Cretaceous extinction. Their exact relationship to crown primates remains debated.

56–34 Ma

Eocene

True primates radiate

Early strepsirrhine- and haplorhine-like primates spread across warm northern forests. Grasping, leaping, and visual specialization flourished.

The age of apes

Quaternary

Many human branches

Multiple Homo lineages lived at the same time, moved across continents, exchanged genes, and eventually left Homo sapiens as the only survivor.

Fossils + genes + anatomy

How do scientists reconstruct a history no one witnessed?

No single kind of evidence is enough. Researchers compare bones and teeth, date rocks, study living primates, reconstruct ancient environments, and use DNA to estimate relationships and divergence times.
