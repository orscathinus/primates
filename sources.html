Evidence & transparency

Sources, uncertainty, and editorial method

This site presents a simplified educational synthesis. It favors ranges over false precision and distinguishes broad consensus from active scientific debate.

Editorial principles

How the information was assembled

01

Prefer authoritative syntheses

Museum research programs, peer-reviewed papers, and university science resources anchor the broad chronology and definitions.

02

Label contested placements

Early primate and hominin fossils are not presented as proven direct ancestors when their exact phylogenetic positions remain debated.

03

Use approximate dates

“Ma” means millions of years ago. Ranges reflect dating uncertainty, population-level divergence, and disagreement among methods.

What might change?

New fossils can change where a taxon sits on the tree. Improved dating can shift its age. Genomic models can revise divergence estimates. Scientific revision is not a flaw in the project—it is how the project advances.
